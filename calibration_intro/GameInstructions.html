Preface: All monetary amounts in this study are shown in Experimental Currency Units (ECU), which will be converted to GBP at the end of the study with an exchange rate of {{ exchange_rate }}.

Environment: The main task of this study involves a closed ring system, meaning you have a predecessor and a successor.

Situation: Everyone has their own inventory that can store units of a virtual good. At any time, you can request one unit of a virtual good from your predecessor. You receive a unit only if your predecessor has one in their inventory. Similarly, your successor can, at any time, request a unit from you.

Inventory: The inventory can be zero or positive. Every time you receive a unit from your predecessor, your inventory increases by one. Every time your successor receives a unit from you, your inventory decreases by one.

Your Actions: To request a unit, click REQUEST in the left box. The box turns blue for a second when your predecessor’s inventory has a unit and you receive it; otherwise, it turns orange.

Your Successor's actions: When your successor requests a unit, the right box turns blue for a second when your inventory has a unit, and your successor receives it; otherwise, it turns orange.

Costs and Benefits:

  • You start with {{ ecu_endowment }} ECUs.
  • You earn {{ ecu_earn }} ECUs every time your successor receives a unit from you.
  • You must pay {{ ecu_inventory_cost }} ECU every second for each unit in your inventory, i.e., holding inventory is costly.
  • You must pay {{ ecu_request_cost }} for every request you make to your predecessor, irrespective of whether it results in you receiving a unit or not.
  • Your ECU balance adjusts accordingly. Technically, it can go negative, but you can also recover.

Duration: The task ends after {{ round_seconds }} seconds ({{ round_minutes }} minutes).
